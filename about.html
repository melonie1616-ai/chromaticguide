About

Who I am and why this site exists.

Who I am

I’m Melonie — a program and project leader who wears a lot of hats: program manager, facilitator, event organizer, operations-minded director, and whatever else the work needs that week. I use AI regularly, but I’m not interested in letting it replace the people part. That part is still the point.

I’m also a musician — French horn and piano. Music taught me early that the interesting stuff often lives between the obvious notes: half-steps, harmony, when to hold a fermata and let the room breathe. That’s where the name Chromatic Guide comes from — not hype, but nuance.

Why this site

I built this site to share thoughts, techniques, and practical resources with others who run programs, meetings, and teams. I want to help people find a sane balance — especially right now, between human judgment and AI capability.

You’ll find things like:

  • Short essays on what to automate and what not to
  • 1-pagers and checklists you can actually use
  • Use cases from real work (genericized — no employer stories)
  • Honest notes on tools I’ve tried

What I believe

AI is a strong second chair — useful for prep, drafts, and clearing friction. It is not the facilitator, the program owner, or the person who holds trust in the room. You are.

Good work is chromatic: not all-or-nothing, not people versus machines — but judgment about where each belongs.

What this site is not

  • Not affiliated with my employer — views here are my own
  • Not “replace your team with AI” content
  • Not a catalog of every new tool — only what I’ve used and found worth sharing

This is a personal site. Content here is written for a general audience and deliberately avoids employer-specific names, tools, or internal details.

By Melonie · Work, like music, lives in the nuance.

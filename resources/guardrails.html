Guardrails

Protecting data and people while you use AI.

Tools

  • Use only approved, secure AI tools for your organization
  • Confirm what licenses and policies your employer provides before adding new apps
  • Check whether AI is already built into tools you use daily (Copilot in M365, etc.)
  • Avoid unverified or “free” tools when content is sensitive or internal

Safe use

  • Never paste or upload sensitive or confidential details into unapproved tools
  • Always double-check AI output before sharing — models make mistakes
  • Watch for bias, especially in hiring, performance, or high-stakes decisions
  • If using open-source or self-hosted AI, involve IT/security — don’t configure it alone

What you can usually upload (within policy)

  • Project plans, status reports, and risk logs
  • Meeting notes or transcripts you’re allowed to store
  • Templates, playbooks, and anonymized data

Best practices

  • Read your organization’s privacy and AI use policies first
  • Use approved platforms for anything that identifies people or projects
  • Give context: your role, audience, and the format you need
  • Review outputs the way you would a junior teammate’s draft — carefully

Speed is never worth a data leak or a decision made on bad AI output. When in doubt, ask IT or compliance — and keep the human review step.
